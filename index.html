About Me

I'm a seismologist, which means I spend my days looking at wiggles on a screen. These wiggles correspond to waves bouncing through the Earth or traveling along its surface, generated by things like earthquakes, explosions, or ocean waves.

Seismologists record these wiggles with high-precision instruments called seismometers. Careful analysis of the wiggles can tell us something about what caused them (sources), or what they traveled through (structure).

I am particuarly interested in understanding Earth structure, characterizing unique seismic sources, deploying instruments in the field, and providing resources to make science more accessible to all.

In my free time, you might find me outside on rocks, ice, snow and rivers.

My Research

My current research interests lie in the field of seismic imaging (specifically, adjoint tomography), which means I use seismic waves and supercomputers to probe the structure of the Earth (think MRI or CT scans).

In adjoint tomography, we generate our own wiggles with computer simulations through 3D Earth models. By systematically changing a starting model, we try to line up the simulated wiggles with the real wiggles, which improves our model, and gives us a better idea about the ground beneath our feet. Neat!
